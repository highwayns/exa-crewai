  • Recession's Grip on IT: Over 129K Professionals Jobless

    The IT sector is facing a looming recession as over 129,000 IT professionals find themselves unemployed. The BLS has adjusted the numbers, indicating a reduction in IT jobs over the past months, with a current unemployment rate for IT pros at 4.7%, higher than the national average.

    The rising unemployment in the IT sector is a troubling indicator of economic challenges, potentially leading to shifts in hiring practices and increased focus on securing job stability. Understanding these trends is crucial for IT professionals navigating their careers during uncertain times.

  • Rippling's Strategic Exclusion in Stock Sale: A Move to Safeguard Information

    Rippling, a HR software startup, has excluded former employees now working for competitors from participating in a lucrative stock sale. This decision follows a giant tender offer meant to provide liquidity to early employees and investors, amidst concerns about sensitive information sharing.

    Rippling's decision underscores the delicate balance companies must maintain between rewarding employees and protecting proprietary information. This case serves as a critical lesson in corporate governance and information security within the HR sector.

  • Evolving Standards in Hiring: Insights from Latest Research

    New research revises the efficacy of various predictors used in hiring, suggesting that structured interviews and job knowledge tests remain highly valid, while advising caution with cognitive ability tests due to potential biases.

    The findings from this research are essential for HR professionals aiming to refine their hiring processes. By understanding which methods yield the most reliable results, companies can improve their selection techniques, leading to better hiring outcomes and reduced bias.
